What is Suicide?

Suicide is an act of ending someone's own life. This is often the result of overwhelming emotional, psychological, or situational distres. People may experience thoughts of suicide due to factors such as bullying, stress, depression, anxiety, trauma, and etc.

Which type of individual/s have a higher chance in committing Suicide?

People who have mental health disorders such as depression and anxiety have a higher chance in committing suicide because they have a sense of hopelessness, where they feel like they are uselss in the world and tend to isolate themselves within groups of people. People who have trauma or abuse such as childhood trauma and sexual abuse can also commit suicide because they think that committing suicide is the only way that they escape the trauma and abuse that they have experienced. People who have an addiction to using substances such as drinking alcohol and using drugs have a chance of committing suicide because addiction these substances can give the user depression because some people use these substances as coping mechanisms for past-trauma and abuse. LGBTQ+ individual/s can also commit suicide because of the discrimination and rejection that the world puts on them for not being the sexual orientation or gender identity which can lead to isoalation and axienty.

What is going on inside the thoughts of someone who is about to commit suicide?

When someone is about to commit suicide, their thoughts are distorted, thus they are unable to think properly and they are unable to talk to anyone. They have a hard time listening to anyone during the critical moment and will have a hard time to really contemplate their choice and if they should go through.

Why don't some people talk about their suicidal thoughts?

People that have suicidal thoughts generally don't like to talk about their thoughts because they fear that they are going to get misjudged and/or misunderstood. They also don't want to burden their loved ones just because of the suicidal thoughts. Another reason is because they feel the thoughts that they are having are unimportant and that no one can help them fix their thoughts. Lastly, they feel that there is lack in awareness and that help is unavailable.

Why don't more people spread awareness about Suicide?

People don't spread awareness because they think that its "cringe" spreading awarness about suicide. They think that they will get bullied if they spread awareness about suicide. People follow the algorythm of social media where they only spread awarenewss when a popular celebrity or popular person posts something on their account about suicide.
